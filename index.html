Ingmar Bergman

Universally ranked among the most accomplished and influential filmmakers of all time

  • 1918: Ingmar Bergman is born on the 14th of July.
  • 1940: Bergman becomes the theatre director for his university.
  • 1941: Ingmar begins writing film scripts as a production assistant.
  • 1944: Ingmar's first film, Torment, is a controversial film about the Swedish education system. The film was heavily criticized by critics and audiences alike. Even his production company, Svensk Filmindustri, fired him.
  • 1949: Bergman achieves domestic success with his 6th feature-length film, Prison.
  • 1953: Bergman meets cinematographer Sven Nykvist, who becomes a long-time collaborator.
  • 1956: Ingmar Bergman breaks out into international acclaim with the release of his 15th feature film, Smiles of a Summer Night.
  • 1957: Ingmar's success has yielded him greater funding and creative freedoms. With his newfound resources, he produces two critically-acclaimed and award-winning films: The Seventh Seal and Wild Strawberries.
  • 1960: Bergman's The Virgin Spring earns him an Academy Award for Best Foreign Language film.
  • 1966: Ingmar Bergman's Persona is released and considered his magnum opus.
  • 1976: Bergman is charged and arrested for income tax evasion. The public ordeal sparks a nervous breakdown and subsequent depression in Bergman, forcing him to self-exile to Munich, Germany. The charges were dismissed because they held no legal basis but Ingmar felt bitter towards Swedish authorities and remained in exile. This leads to public outcry in Sweden; Even the Swedish Prime Minister asked him to return. The ordeal had an estimated cost of 10 million SEK and led to the termination of hundreds of jobs in the Swedish film industry.
  • 1980: Ingmar Bergman begins producing films in Munich.
  • 1984: Ingmar Bergman returns to Sweden and creates a number of projects in television and theatre.
  • 2007: Ingmar Bergman dies on 30 July.
